Intro

A lot goes behind how food gets to the plate, and as technology is rapidly advancing, big tech on farms is the future of sustainability and animal nutrition.

What is Nutreco?

Nutreco is a leader in animal nutrition and plans on using technology to feed the growing population in a sustainable manner. There are several locations across Canada and internationally, in Ontario the two locations are in St Marys and Puslinch.

In Nutreco, everyone plays an important role in ensuring customers produce as much feed as possible for Feeding the Future. Nutreco offers a wide range of services/products with animal feed being at the core of most of them.

My Goals at Nutreco

I had set a list of goals both technical and non-technical, to ensure that I can learn as much as possible during my time as an intern.

My goals included the following,

1. Learn how the cloud-based application Amino works and how to use it effectively with M3.

2. Communicating the bigger picture of Amino as well as its finer details to an audience of a wide range of technical prophecies.

3. Working on a real Project meant meeting deadlines – more specifically providing training on how to use Amino during summer and start UAT (User Acceptance Testing) in fall to go live by the end of the year.

4. Getting provinces across Canada on one application can prove to be difficult if regional business practices and nuisances are taken into consideration. So, learning and applying that into my day to day work was important.

5. Understanding the supply chain of delivering feed to birds, and the greater picture of big tech on multiple sites.

These goals are central to my current job’s day to day, and beyond that as well, as they will continue to help me in future positions. More specifically, learning how to articulate technical concepts to both tech savvy and non-technical professionals. That skill will provide me the confidence in future roles that will be more technical.

Learning how to read errors that APIs can throw and how to interpret those errors to quickly think of solutions, has been beneficial for practicing how to solve problems. Having different kinds of methods for solving problems would be crucial in a technical position such as a developer. Documenting solved errors/problems has also been an efficient way to develop good practices for debugging.

I also focused on how to look for information in the correct places. Working with such a complex application there is a lot of documentation available, however, knowing which pieces of information is useful for my situation can save time and increase productivity. The skill of knowing how to search for information given a lot of documents provides me confidence in learning new technologies and concepts. As many companies tend to write long and detailed documentation about their products and software, knowing how to go through that is essential.

Outside of the Amino and M3 applications, I had the opportunity to learn more about Microsoft Power Apps, Power Automate, Copilot Studios, and Dataverse. Exploring these topics through assigned learnings diversified my technical experience during my coop placement thus far. I want to be able to work with these tools for smaller projects so that I can apply them to a real-world situation. This would be an interesting experience as a Computer Science student so that I am able to compare creating applications/solutions to problems via programming vs. low code solutions.

Lastly, a huge chunk of my work involved creating documentation for troubleshooting problems and using Aminos features. These documents were then attached to a Copilot AI Bot, to answer user questions about Amino. Testing to see how to an AI Bot reacts to prompts as someone who wrote the documents was a great learning opportunity. I learned what formats of writing work best for AI to answer general “How to” questions, and how it has trouble answering more vague and open-ended questions. My goal for the Amino Bot is to add bigger and more in-depth paragraphs about what Amino is, why it is important, and how to best approach it. As well as, additional terminology users may use depending on the region that means the same thing across Canada, so that knowledge of Amino terms is not necessary to ask the Amino Bot questions.

Conclusion

It’s very important to be aware of what goes on behind the scenes of getting food to consumers. It takes a lot of people across many professions to ensure quality food is sold. Using technology to optimize and enhance that process is the future of animal nutrition.
